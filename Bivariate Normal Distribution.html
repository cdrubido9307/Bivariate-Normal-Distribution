Bivariate Normal distribution¶

$$ f(x, y) = \frac{1}{2 \pi \sigma_X \sigma_Y \sqrt{1 - \rho^2}} e^{-\frac{1}{2 \left(1 - \rho^2\right)} \left( \left( \frac{x - \mu_X}{\sigma_X} \right)^{2} + \left(\frac{y - \mu_Y}{\sigma_Y}\right) ^{2} - 2 \rho \frac{\left(x - \mu_X\right)\left(y - \mu_Y\right)}{\sigma_X \sigma_Y}\right)} $$

Consider, $$ \mu_X = 0 $$ $$ \mu_Y = 0 $$ $$ \sigma_X^{2} = 1 $$ $$ \sigma_Y^{2} = 1 $$ $$ \rho = \frac{1}{2} $$

Then we have, $$ f(x, y) = \frac{1}{2 \pi \sqrt{1 - \left( \frac{1}{2} \right) ^ 2}} e^{-\frac{1}{2\left(1 - \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2\right)} \left(x^{2} + y^{2} - xy \right)} $$
